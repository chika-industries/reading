# Summary of Nomad Investment Partnership's First Year (September 2001 - December 2002)

## Launch and Initial Context
The Nomad Investment Partnership launched in early September 2001 and began investing on September 10th—fortuitously timed just before markets fell. The fund started with approximately 28% cash holdings, allowing Sleep to invest significantly at depressed prices.

## Performance
- **September 10 to December 2001:** +10.1% (vs MSCI World +3.9%)
- **2002:** +1.3% (vs MSCI World -19.54%)
- **Since inception (10 Sept 2001 to Dec 2002):** +11.57% (vs index -16.28%)

Sleep emphasizes that value investors should expect better-than-average performance in declining markets and poorer-than-average in rising markets—a pattern already visible.

## Investment Philosophy
- Target businesses trading at **~50% of real business value**
- Seek **owner-oriented management** with sensible capital allocation
- Global mandate allows hunting anywhere for mispriced opportunities
- No leverage, shorting, or derivatives—"old-fashioned stock picking"
- Average holding period targeted at ~10 years
- Patience of the investor base is a key competitive advantage

## Notable Holdings Discussed

**International Speedway (ISCA):** Nascar racetrack operator. Bought at ~half peak price, valuing the business at low single-digit growth when Sleep expected much more.

**Matichon (Thailand):** Second-largest Thai newspaper. Valued at 0.75x revenues or ~4x normalized free cash flow—about one-third of estimated worth.

**Xerox:** Accounting scandal had crushed shares, but cash flow was unaffected. Sleep argued the business was worth ~$14/share vs purchase at ~$6.

**Stagecoach:** UK bus operator that had overexpanded. Brian Souter returned to implement "cancer surgery"—cutting weak businesses. Purchased at 14p, valued at ~60p.

**Costco Wholesale:** "Scale efficiencies shared" model. Purchased at ~$30 when Sleep saw fair value above $50.

## Mistakes Acknowledged
- **Conseco:** Filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy—investment became near worthless
- **Monsanto:** Sold after discovering hidden environmental indemnification liabilities (fortunate timing—shares later halved)

## Key Quotes on Patience
> "If Nomad is to have a competitive advantage over our peers this will come from the capital allocation skills of your manager (if any) and the patience of our investor base. Only by looking further out than the short-term crowd can we expect to beat them."

By year-end, the fund was ~95% invested with price-to-value ratio around 50 cents on the dollar. No investor turnover and almost no performance inquiries despite market declines.
