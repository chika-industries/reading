## The Performance Fee Hurdle: When 6% is Not 6%

Sleep reveals that Nomad's 6% performance fee hurdle has actually been "well over ten percent in the recent past." Why?

Two reasons:
1. A 10% loss followed by a 10% gain is not mathematically equivalent to zero (the math works against the hurdle)
2. The performance fee reserve is itself invested in Nomad shares, so it effectively "buys high (accrue) and sells low (reimburse)"

Zak has set himself the task of finding a case when the 6% hurdle might be less than 6%. "He may be some time."

Sleep admits they didn't foresee the accounting complexity when they pencilled the fee structure on a napkin in a California hotel bar a decade ago. But if someone had pointed out the issues, "I don't think we would have changed anything."

## Non-Monetary Incentives & The Meaning of Work

Sleep draws on Dan Ariely's research (TED, Buenos Aires, 2012) on motivation.

**The Lego robot experiment:**
- "Rewards group": finished robots placed on a shelf for all to see → built 50% more robots at much lower pay
- "Sisypheans": finished robots broken up in front of the builder → stopped at much higher pay

Conclusion: having their models destroyed took away the meaning of their work. The lack of meaning was not outweighed by the pay.

**The cake mix story (1940s):**
- Powdered cake mix (just add water) → terrible sales
- New product requiring fresh eggs, milk, sugar → sales improved
- Putting in one's own effort adds perceived value (the Ikea effect)

**Origami experiment:**
- Builders paid more to keep their own (inferior) origami than professional models would cost
- Those who built without instruction (worst origami) were willing to pay the most

## Application to Entrepreneurs

The best entrepreneurs Sleep knows don't particularly care about compensation packages:
- Jeff Bezos and Warren Buffett have waived salaries, bonuses, options
- Nick Robertson (Asos) — asked if he thinks of leaving now that he's rich: his face lights up with future possibilities; "he is having more fun now than ever before"
- Jim Sinegal (Costco), Lord Harris (Carpetright) — derive meaning from challenge, identity, creativity, ethos

> "Financial incentives may be necessary, but they may also not be sufficient in themselves to bring out the best in people."

Sleep is relaxed about "6% not being 6%" because they derive great value from the meaning of their work — not just financial rewards.

## Inactivity as Value Added

Portfolio inaction continues: purchase and sale transactions have "all but ground to a halt."

At the time of initial investment, Nomad's investee businesses were on average ~15 years old (excluding Berkshire and Costco: ~12 years). The average S&P 500 constituent has been in the index for 25 years — suggesting those firms might be ~20 years old on inclusion. Nomad's firms are very youthful.

> "The runway ahead for our businesses may be very long indeed."

Sleep jokes about a planned "Nomad Inactivity App" (available only in the Amazon App store, of course). He quotes Charlie Munger: "You make your real money sitting on your assets!"
