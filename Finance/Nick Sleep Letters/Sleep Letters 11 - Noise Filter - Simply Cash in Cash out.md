# Summary of Nomad Investment Partnership's 2011 Calendar Year (Annual Letter to December 31, 2011)

## Performance

- **2011 calendar year:** -9.9% (vs MSCI World -5.5%)
- **Since inception (Sept 2001 to Dec 2011):** +347.6% before fees, +13.6% annualized after fees (vs index +47.9% cumulative, +3.9% annualized)

A dollar invested at inception was worth **$3.71 after fees** as of January 1, 2012, vs $1.48 for the index.

Sleep notes the decline was a "down year" but remains focused on the long-term destination.

## Keep It Simple: Cash-In, Cash-Out

Sleep recalls a lesson from early in his career: as a young analyst, he asked an overly complicated question to a US bank CFO. The CFO replied: "Look, son, at the end of the day it is all about cash-in, and cash-out."

Nomad runs a single partnership with long-term investments in ~10 companies, all paid for with cash. "It is terribly, terribly simple, but it is not easy." Distractions abound: news, macroeconomics, politics, regulation, compliance — the list is not exhaustive.

## The Question That Matters

One trick to filter noise: **"Does any of this make a meaningful difference to the relationship our businesses have with their customers?"**

This bond between customers and companies is one of the most important factors in determining long-term success.

For Colgate-Palmolive (50x return over 30 years), what mattered was the economics of line extensions and advertising psychology. For Wal-Mart and Costco, it's scale savings shared with customers. The rest — stock market crises, management changes — was mostly noise.

## Two Ways to Build a Successful Company (Jeff Bezos)

> "There are two ways to build a successful company. One is to work very, very hard to convince customers to pay high margins [Colgate, Nike, Coca-Cola model]. The other is to work very, very hard to be able to offer customers low margins [Wal-Mart, Costco, AirAsia, Amazon, Asos model]. They both work. We're firmly in the second camp."

Bezos prefers large customer base with low margins because sharing efficiency benefits turns size (normally an anchor) into an asset. The moat deepens as the firm grows.

**Evidence:** Weighted average revenue growth of Nomad's firms is currently >30% organic, with falling prices, in a time of austerity. Return on capital is over twice that of competing businesses.

## The Real Risks Are Not in the Newspapers

The risks that could destroy Nomad's firms are not credit crises, housing crises, or Euro crises. They are events that could prevent the firms from reaching their potential:

- Wide-scale credit card fraud followed by a **disingenuous response** (breaking the bond of trust)
- Success itself: bubble valuations or political risk (e.g., an online retailing tax)

The founder of AirAsia, when asked what he would do if his firm put a plane in a swamp, raised both hands in a gesture of having nothing to hide. "That response strikes us as very healthy."

## Black Arrow Group: When Zero Pays a Dividend

Black Arrow Group (de-listed, Arnold brothers own ~88%) was valued at nil in Nomad's accounts. Yet it recently declared a dividend of 1p per share. "The Alchemists were not that good, so what has been going on?" Sleep explains: the business is troubled but not worthless; but assigning a positive value would be arbitrary as they cannot give cash for shares. So zero it is — and they'll enjoy the dividend alchemy while it lasts.

## The School Turnaround Project

Sleep has been a governor of a failed school in South East London. After becoming an "academy" (free from local authority control), the school will soon turn an 85% failure rate into an 85% pass rate — with **no extra funding**. The school's revenues match those of its failed predecessor.

> "It can be tempting to think that more money is the answer to problems but it strikes us that it is not the money you have, but the choices you make, that count."

## Bezos on Time Horizons

> "Our first shareholder letter, in 1997, was entitled, 'It's all about the long-term.' If everything you do needs to work on a three-year time horizon, then you're competing against a lot of people. But if you are willing to invest on a seven-year time horizon, you're now competing against a fraction of those people... Just by lengthening the time horizon, you can engage in endeavours that you could never otherwise pursue."

Amazon likes things to work in five to seven years. "We're stubborn on the vision and flexible on the details."

## The Uniqueness of Nomad's Ecosystem

> "The uniqueness of Nomad's ecosystem is the look-through consistency of approach of its participants, from Mr. Bezos and the good folks that run Nomad's other businesses, to Zak and me and on to our investing partners. We are all choosing to see the world in the same way. It is very simple and, because it is the road-less-travelled, it is also very valuable."

Sleep reminds Partners never to underestimate their role. "It is for that reason we named Nomad a Partnership, not a fund."
