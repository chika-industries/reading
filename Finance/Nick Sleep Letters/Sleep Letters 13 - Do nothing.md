## Lord Harris & The School Turnaround

Sleep describes visiting Carpetright founder Lord Harris. Asked what motivates him at 66: "Oh, you should see the academies."

When UK schools fail, oversight is given to independent bodies like Lord Harris' Federation of Academies. The school Sleep became involved with had an 85% exam failure rate. With **no extra funding** (funding per pupil is ~1/3 of private day schools), after five years that 85% failure rate will soon become an 85% pass rate.

How? Every penny watched. Resources spent on the right things: bonuses for teacher attendance and improved results, extra-curricular activities. The predecessor school had Friday afternoons off. The academy does not.

## Zak's Family Activity Centre (Real-World Investing)

Zak's relatives opened a children's activity centre (climbing structures, slides, restaurant). Key lessons:

- Paying above minimum wage attracted **better staff** (nursery assistants who cared about children)
- Polling revealed **parents** made the decision, not child nagging. Parents care about cleanliness, new fittings, good coffee.
- Pricing kept just under competition, not ruinously low
- After two competitors closed (one run by hired hands, not proprietors), revenues jumped 25%

Zak's counter-intuitive advice to his relatives: **take prices down** now. Super-normal profits attract new competitors. Better to earn less, but for much longer. The family agreed.

> "It has been fun for Zak and me to practice in the real world, what we preach in investing."
