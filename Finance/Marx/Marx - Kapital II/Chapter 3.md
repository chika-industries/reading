# Summary of Chapter 3: The Circuit of Commodity-Capital

## General Formula

The circuit of commodity-capital is: **C' → M' → C ... P ... C'**

Here, C' is not just the product but also the premise of the previous two circuits. It appears as the starting point, and the circuit closes with C' again (or C'' if reproduction is expanded).

## Distinctive Features

Unlike the circuits of money-capital (M ... M') and productive capital (P ... P), the commodity-capital circuit:

1. **Opens with the total circulation** (C' → M' → C) before production (P) intervenes
2. **Starts with self-expanded capital** – already augmented by surplus-value – not merely advanced capital-value
3. **Includes both productive and individual consumption** as conditions of the circuit

## Separation of Capital-Value and Surplus-Value

C' consists of capital-value (C) plus surplus-value (c). In the act C' → M', these become separable:

- The capitalist can sell portions of the product successively
- For example, selling first the part equal to constant capital, then variable capital, then surplus-value
- This allows the capital-value to begin its new circuit before the surplus-value is fully realised

## Importance for Social Reproduction

C' ... C' is the only circuit where:

- The starting point is the *total* commodity-product (including surplus-value)
- The expenditure of surplus-value as revenue is explicitly included
- Individual consumption enters as a necessary condition

This form is the groundwork for **Quesnay's *Tableau économique*** – the first systematic analysis of social reproduction.

## Relation to Social Capital

- C' ... C' is not just the circuit of an individual capital but also reflects the movement of **aggregate social capital**
- It reveals the intertwining of individual capitals and their replacement of one another
- The problems solved here are assumed rather than demonstrated when studying isolated individual capitals

## Practical Application

This form appears naturally in agriculture, where calculations run from harvest to harvest (the *reprise* of the physiocrats, as opposed to the *avance* of productive capital).
