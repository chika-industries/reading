# Summary of Chapter 2: The Circuit of Productive Capital

## General Formula

The circuit of productive capital is: **P ... C' → M' → C ... P**

This represents the periodic renewal of productive capital's functioning—its reproduction as a process aimed at self-expansion of value. Unlike the money-capital circuit, here the entire circulation process (C' → M' → C) appears merely as an interruption between productive capital at the start and its return to the same form.

## I. Simple Reproduction

The circulation process within the circuit is C' → M' → C, which takes the form of simple commodity circulation: C → M → C (sell to buy), the opposite of money-capital's M → C → M (buy to sell).

### Key Points:

- **C' (commodity-capital)** = C (capital-value) + c (surplus-value)
- After C' → M', the capital-value (M) and surplus-value (m) become separable sums of money
- **M → C** reconverts money into productive capital (means of production + labor-power)
- **m → c** is the circulation of the capitalist's revenue (surplus-value spent on personal consumption)

### Important Observations:

- The money advanced for wages (M → L) is actually the converted equivalent of a part of the commodity-value the worker himself produced
- For the worker, the transaction is L → M → C (sell labor-power, buy necessities) – a simple commodity circulation
- For the capitalist, the circuit is C → M → C – but this mediates the renewal of productive capital

### Crisis and Over-production:

- Commodity-capitals compete for market space
- A crisis becomes visible not in decreased consumer demand, but in the **decrease of exchanges of capital for capital** – the disruption of capital's reproductive process
- Commodities may be "absorbed only apparently" by consumption, remaining unsold with dealers

## II. Accumulation and Reproduction on an Extended Scale

- Surplus-value intended for capitalisation often must accumulate through several circuits before reaching sufficient size to function as additional capital
- This forms **latent money-capital** – surplus-value congealed into a hoard
- Latent money-capital is not yet functioning capital; it's a preliminary stage of accumulation

The general formula for productive capital's circuit, combining simple and extended reproduction:

**P ... C' → M' → C' ... P'**

(P' indicates productive capital reproduced on an enlarged scale with greater value)

## III. Accumulation of Money

- Surplus-value turned into money may require a minimum size before it can be added to productive capital (e.g., to buy additional spindles, carders, etc.)
- While accumulating to this minimum, it exists as a **hoard** – latent money-capital
- Hoarding appears as a temporary accompaniment to real accumulation

## IV. Reserve Fund

- The hoard can also serve as a **reserve fund** to counter disturbances in the circuit (e.g., delayed sales, rising prices of means of production)
- This differs from the normal money reserve that is part of functioning capital
- The reserve fund is surplus-value not yet transformed into active capital, but available for emergencies

## Final Formula

The circuit of productive capital is the form in which classical Political Economy examined industrial capital's circular movement.
