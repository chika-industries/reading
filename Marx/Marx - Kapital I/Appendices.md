After Chapter 33, *Capital*, Volume 1 includes the following sections:

1.  **Appendix: "Results of the Immediate Process of Production"** – This is a lengthy, unfinished manuscript (about 140 pages) that Marx originally intended to be part of Volume 1. It was published separately later. It covers:
    - **Commodities as the Product of Capital** – Further analysis of how the commodity form develops under capitalism, emphasizing that the product is always the result of capital's self-valorization.
    - **Capitalist Production as the Production of Surplus-Value** – A deeper exploration of the production process, the distinction between constant and variable capital, and the fetishism of capital.
    - **Capitalist Production as the Production and Reproduction of the Specifically Capitalist Relations of Production** – How capitalism reproduces not only commodities but also the class relation between capital and wage-labour.
    - **Isolated Fragments** – Assorted notes and unfinished thoughts.

2.  **Quotations in Languages Other Than English and German** – A list of translated passages.

3.  **Index of Authorities Quoted** – A bibliography of authors Marx cited.

4.  **General Index** – A detailed subject index.

5.  **Note on Previous Editions of the Works of Marx and Engels** – Bibliographical information.

6.  **Chronology of Works by Marx and Engels** – A timeline of their major writings.

The appendix is valuable for understanding Marx's later refinements, but it is not essential for following the main argument of Volume 1. The rest are scholarly apparatus.
