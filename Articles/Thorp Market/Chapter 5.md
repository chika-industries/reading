# Summary of Chapter 5: "The System in Action – $100,000 Doubles"

This chapter details the actual investments made by Sheen Kassouf and his brothers using the basic system, showing how they achieved about 25% annual returns over five years.

## The Molybdenum Story (1961–1963)

- Initial position: long 150 shares of Molybdenum common (average $33), short 400 warrants (average $18). Warrants expired in 22 months, exercisable at $28.83. Total investment ~$8,500.
- The profit profile showed no loss unless the common rose above $52; even if common became worthless, the investment would return 26%.
- In early 1962, as the stock market weakened, they increased the short position to 1,300 warrants while remaining long 150 common.
- In April 1962, a “short squeeze” rumor drove the warrant from 16⅞ to 25 in a few days. International Mining and Kennecott Copper held large blocks, but the squeeze failed.
- By early 1963, with the warrant below normal price, they closed the position. Average monthly cash investment: $11,500; total profit: $6,435 → **56% return in 16 months (≈42% per annum)**.
- Later, when the warrant again became overpriced, they shorted 100 warrants at 7⅝. The AMEX banned further short sales, but they held until just before expiration and covered at $1 – a 6‑month gain of $650 on a $500 investment.

## Bunker‑Ramo (Teleregister) (1963–1965)

- In April 1963, they bought 1,000 shares of Teleregister common at 4½ and shorted 1,000 warrants at 2⅝. Total investment: $5,041.
- The profit profile showed no loss if the stock advanced, and loss only if common fell below 2⅛.
- Using the avalanche effect, they pyramided short sales as warrants declined. In one separate account, shorting 400 warrants at 5⅛, they later added 200 more warrants at 3⅝ without additional margin.
- Over 26 months, average monthly investment: $3,500; total profit: $8,964 → **250% total return (≈120% per annum)**.

## Sperry Rand (1962–1966)

- In September 1962, Sperry common was $14, warrants $8, exercisable at $25 (later $28), expiring in 5 years.
- They projected that even if common fell to $6 (a disaster scenario), the hedge would break even. If common stayed at $14, profit would be $800 on the short warrants.
- They built a position over time: about 5,000 common shares (mostly below $15) and 7,500 warrants short (mostly above $8).
- As the common rose in 1964–1965, they sold common and shorted additional warrants without adding funds. When common reached 28 in July 1966, warrants lagged at $10 (below normal price). They closed the position.
- Over 47 months, average monthly investment: $40,000; profit: ~$35,000.

## Overall Results

- Total net profits from these three situations: **$66,200**.
- Including other situations (National Tea, Universal American, Pacific Petroleums, Realty Equities), total profits reached about **$85,000 by October 1966**.
- The authors earned **more than 25% per year** on their investments over the five‑year period.
- Friends and clients who followed similar investments had even larger totals, with many hundreds of thousands of dollars effectively doubled.

The chapter emphasizes that the system worked through both bull and bear markets, and that active management (adjusting mixes, pyramiding, and closing positions when warrants became fairly priced) significantly enhanced returns.
