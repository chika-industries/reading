
### Part 4 Summary: Buffett’s Alpha and Investment Style – Factor Analysis

The authors ask: *What types of stocks does Buffett buy?* To answer this, they run time‑series regressions of Berkshire’s excess returns on a set of well‑known factors.

#### Regression model

\[
r_t - r_t^f = \alpha + \beta_1 \text{MKT}_t + \beta_2 \text{SMB}_t + \beta_3 \text{HML}_t + \beta_4 \text{UMD}_t + \beta_5 \text{BAB}_t + \beta_6 \text{QMJ}_t + \epsilon_t
\]

Where:
- **MKT** = excess return of the overall equity market
- **SMB** = size factor (Small minus Big) – buying small caps, shorting large caps
- **HML** = value factor (High book‑to‑market minus Low) – buying cheap (value) stocks
- **UMD** = momentum factor (Up minus Down) – buying past winners, shorting losers
- **BAB** = Betting Against Beta (Frazzini & Pedersen 2014) – buying low‑beta (safe) stocks, shorting high‑beta (risky) stocks
- **QMJ** = Quality Minus Junk (Asness, Frazzini & Pedersen, forthcoming) – buying high‑quality stocks (profitable, growing, safe, high payout)

#### Key findings (Table 4 in the paper)

1. **Berkshire’s market beta is low (0.69)** – Buffett buys relatively safe stocks.

2. **Standard factors (MKT, SMB, HML, UMD) do not explain Buffett’s alpha.**  
   - The alpha remains large and statistically significant after controlling for these four factors.

3. **Adding BAB and QMJ makes the alpha insignificant.**  
   - For Berkshire’s **public stock portfolio**, the alpha drops to an annualized **0.3%** (statistically not different from zero).  
   - For Berkshire’s overall stock return, the alpha drops by about half and becomes insignificant in most specifications.  
   - The private holdings also show reduced alpha, though estimates are noisier.

4. **Berkshire loads significantly and positively on:**
   - **HML (value)** – Buffett buys cheap stocks (high book‑to‑market).  
   - **BAB (betting against beta)** – Buffett buys safe, low‑beta stocks.  
   - **QMJ (quality)** – Buffett buys high‑quality companies (profitable, stable, growing).  
   - **Negative loading on SMB** – Buffett buys large‑cap stocks (not small caps).  
   - **Insignificant loading on UMD** – Buffett does not chase momentum.

#### Interpretation

- Buffett is known as the “ultimate value investor,” but **his focus on safe, high‑quality stocks is at least as important** as value.  
- The authors quote Buffett’s own words from the 2008 Annual Report:  
  > “Whether we’re talking about socks or stocks, I like buying quality merchandise when it is marked down.”

- The fact that BAB and QMJ (factors identified decades after Buffett started investing) explain his performance **does not diminish his accomplishment**. He recognized early that these themes work, stuck with them, and applied cheap leverage.

#### Practical takeaway

A significant part of Buffett’s “secret” is simply:
> **Buy cheap (value), safe (low beta), high‑quality stocks, and use modest, stable leverage.**
