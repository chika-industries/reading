Here are the summaries for Articles 6 through 15 of the U.S.-Japan Tax Treaty.

### Article 6: Income from Real Property
- **Taxing Right:** Income derived by a resident of one country (e.g., Japan) from real property (land, buildings) located in the other country (e.g., the U.S.) **may be taxed in that other country** (the source country).
- **Definition:** The term "real property" has the meaning under the law of the country where the property is situated. It includes accessories, livestock/equipment used in agriculture, and rights to variable payments for working mineral deposits. Ships and aircraft are explicitly excluded.
- **Scope:** This applies to income from direct use, leasing, or any other form of exploitation of real property.

### Article 7: Business Profits
- **Primary Rule:** The profits of an enterprise (e.g., a Japanese company) are taxable **only in its country of residence** (Japan) unless it carries on business in the other country (U.S.) through a **Permanent Establishment (PE)** located there.
- **PE Taxation:** If a PE exists, the other country (U.S.) may tax the profits attributable to that PE.
- **Attribution:** Profits are attributed to the PE as if it were a distinct and separate enterprise dealing independently with the head office. Expenses (including executive and general administrative expenses) incurred for the PE are deductible.
- **No "Force of Attraction":** A country cannot tax all of a foreign enterprise's income just because the enterprise has a PE there; it can only tax the income attributable to that PE.

### Article 8: Shipping and Air Transport
- **Exclusive Taxation:** Profits from the operation of ships or aircraft in **international traffic** by an enterprise of a Contracting State (e.g., Japan Airlines) are taxable **only in that State** (Japan).
- **Inclusions:** Profits include:
    - Rental of ships/aircraft on a full (crewed) basis.
    - Bareboat (uncrewed) rental if incidental to international traffic operations.
    - Inland transport of goods/passengers if it is part of international traffic.
- **Containers:** Profits from the use, maintenance, or rental of containers (and related equipment) are taxable only in the enterprise's country of residence, unless the containers are used solely within the other country.

### Article 9: Associated Enterprises (Transfer Pricing)
- **Arm's Length Principle:** If related enterprises (e.g., parent and subsidiary) have financial or commercial relations that differ from those that independent enterprises would have, profits that would have accrued to one enterprise but did not because of those conditions **may be included in the profits of that enterprise** and taxed accordingly.
- **Correlative Adjustments:** If one country adjusts a company's profits upward, and the other country agrees the adjustment was correct, that other country **must make an appropriate adjustment** to relieve double taxation on the other related company.
- **Time Limit:** A Contracting State generally **cannot** adjust a company's profits if the examination to do so is initiated more than **seven years** after the end of the taxable year in which the profits would have accrued (except in cases of fraud or willful default).

### Article 10: Dividends
- **Taxation in Residence State:** Dividends paid by a company resident in one country to a resident of the other country may be taxed in the recipient's country of residence.
- **Limitation in Source State:** The source country (where the payer resides) can also tax, but the rate is limited:
    - **5%** of the gross amount if the beneficial owner is a company owning directly or indirectly at least **10%** of the voting stock of the payer.
    - **10%** of the gross amount in all other cases.
- **Full Exemption (0%):** The source country shall **not** tax dividends if:
    - The beneficial owner is a company that has owned more than **50%** of the voting stock for 12 months and qualifies for specific treaty benefits (limitation on benefits provisions); or
    - The beneficial owner is a **Pension Fund** (and the dividends are not derived from a business).
- **Special Entities:** Special rules apply to dividends from U.S. RICs and REITs, as well as Japanese companies entitled to a dividends-paid deduction (similar to REITs). Generally, the 5% rate does not apply to these entities.
- **PE Exception:** The reduced rates do not apply if the shareholder has a PE in the source country and the shareholding is effectively connected to that PE; instead, Article 7 (Business Profits) applies.

### Article 11: Interest
- **Taxation in Residence State:** Interest arising in one country and paid to a resident of the other may be taxed in the recipient's country.
- **Limitation in Source State:** The source country may tax, but the rate is limited to **10%** of the gross amount.
- **Full Exemption (0%):** Interest is taxable **only** in the residence country if it is beneficially owned by:
    - The other State itself, its central bank, or a wholly-owned institution (e.g., Bank of Japan, U.S. Export-Import Bank).
    - A resident with respect to debt-claims guaranteed/insured by that other State.
    - Certain qualifying financial institutions (banks, insurance companies, securities dealers) or other enterprises that meet specific bond-issuance or deposit-taking criteria.
- **Excess Interest:** If a special relationship between the payer and beneficiary causes the interest amount to exceed an arm's length amount, the excess may be taxed at **5%** (rather than the normal 10% or 0%).

### Article 12: Royalties
- **Exclusive Taxation:** Royalties arising in one country and beneficially owned by a resident of the other country shall be taxable **only in that other country** (the residence country). The source country generally gives up its right to tax.
- **Definition:** Royalties include payments for the use of, or right to use, copyrights, patents, trademarks, designs, secret processes, industrial equipment, and know-how.
- **PE Exception:** The exclusive residence-state taxation does not apply if the beneficiary has a PE in the source country and the royalty right is effectively connected to that PE; instead, Article 7 applies.
- **Excess Royalties:** If a special relationship causes the royalty amount to exceed an arm's length amount, the excess may be taxed in the source country at a rate of **5%**.
- **Anti-Abuse:** A resident cannot be considered the beneficial owner if the payment is structured to pass through them to a third person not entitled to treaty benefits.

### Article 13: Gains from Alienation of Property (Capital Gains)
- **Real Property:** Gains from the sale of real property located in a country may be taxed in that country.
- **Shares of Real Property-Rich Companies:** Gains from the sale of shares in a company resident in the other country may be taxed there if the company derives **at least 50% of its value** from real property located there. *Exception:* This does not apply if the shares are publicly traded and the seller owns 5% or less of that class.
- **PE Assets:** Gains from the sale of movable property forming part of a PE (or the PE itself) may be taxed in the country where the PE is located.
- **Ships/Aircraft/Containers:** Gains from the sale of ships, aircraft, or containers used in international traffic are taxable **only** in the residence country of the enterprise.
- **Residual:** Gains from any other property not mentioned above are taxable only in the seller's country of residence.

### Article 14: Income from Employment (Salaries and Wages)
- **General Rule:** Employment income is taxable **only in the employee's country of residence**.
- **Source-Country Taxation:** The country where the employment is exercised may tax the income **unless** all three of the following conditions are met (the "183-day rule"):
    1.  The employee is present in the work country for **183 days or less** in any 12-month period; and
    2.  The employer is **not a resident** of the work country; and
    3.  The remuneration is **not borne** by a PE of the employer in the work country.
- **Crews:** Remuneration for employment aboard ships or aircraft operated in international traffic may be taxed in the country of residence of the enterprise operating the vessel.

### Article 15: Directors' Fees
- **Taxing Right:** Directors' fees and other similar payments derived by a resident of one country (e.g., Japan) in their capacity as a member of the board of directors of a company resident in the other country (e.g., U.S.) **may be taxed in that other country** (where the company is resident).
