### Chapter 3: Major Taxes in Japan Summary
This chapter provides a matrix categorizing Japanese taxes by type (National vs. Local) and by what they are levied on.

The major taxes are organized as follows:

- **Taxes on profit and income:**
    - *National:* Income tax, Corporate tax
    - *Prefectural:* Inhabitants tax, Enterprise tax
    - *Municipal:* Inhabitants tax
- **Taxes on gifts and inheritance:**
    - *National:* Gift tax, Inheritance tax
- **Taxes on properties:**
    - *Prefectural:* Automobile tax
    - *Municipal:* Property tax, Special landholding tax, City planning tax, Light motor vehicle tax, Business office tax
- **Taxes on consumption:**
    - *National:* Consumption tax
    - *Prefectural:* Golf links tax
- **Taxes on transactions:**
    - *National:* Stamp tax, Registration and license tax
    - *Prefectural:* Real property acquisition tax, Automobile acquisition tax

### Chapter 4: Taxes on Income Summary
This chapter details the taxation of income for both individuals and corporations, as well as the withholding tax system.

#### (1) Taxes on Individuals' Income
- **Income Tax (National):**
    - Income is categorized into **10 types** (e.g., interest, salary, business, retirement).
    - The tax year runs from January to December, with final returns filed **February 16 to March 15** of the following year.
    - *Who must file:* Those earning over 짜20 million, those with secondary income over 짜200,000, or those with multiple employers.
    - *Residential Status:* Tax liability depends on status:
        - **Permanent Resident**: Taxed on all worldwide income.
        - **Non-Permanent Resident**: Taxed on all income except foreign-source income paid abroad (for those with Japanese residency under 5 years).
        - **Nonresident**: Taxed only on Japanese-source income.
- **Inhabitants Tax (Local):**
    - A per-capita and per-income tax levied by prefectures and municipalities on individuals residing or having an office in the jurisdiction as of January 1st. It is based on the previous year's income.

#### (2) Taxes on Corporate Income
- **Corporate Tax (National):**
    - Levied on the income of corporations.
    - *Domestic Corporations:* Taxed on worldwide income.
    - *Foreign Corporations:* Taxed only on Japanese-source income.
    - *Tax Rate:* Generally **30%** ; small companies (capital under 짜100 million) pay **22%** on the first 짜8 million of income.
    - Returns are due within **two months** of the business year-end.
    - **Transfer Pricing:** Deals with foreign partners must reflect "arm's length" prices.
- **Corporate Inhabitants Tax (Local):** Levied by prefectures/municipalities where the corporation has offices.
- **Corporate Enterprise Tax (Prefectural):** Imposed on businesses operating in the prefecture.

#### (3) Withholding Income Tax
- When paying individuals or corporations, entities must often withhold tax and pay it to the tax office.
- **For Residents:** Withholding applies to interest, dividends, salaries, and retirement allowances. Salaries are taxed at progressive rates; others are capped at **20%** .
- **For Nonresidents:** The rules are more complex.
    - Those **with** a permanent establishment in Japan are generally taxed on business income.
    - Those **without** a permanent establishment are usually subject to a final **withholding tax** on specific Japanese-source income (e.g., 20% on interest, dividends, or service fees; 10% on land transfers).
- **Tax Treaties:** Japan has treaties with 56 countries that may reduce these rates. Taxpayers must submit notifications to claim these relief measures.
