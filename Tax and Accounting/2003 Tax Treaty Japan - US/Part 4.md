Here are the summaries for the remaining articles, **Articles 26 through 31**, which cover the administrative provisions, entry into force, and termination of the treaty.

### Article 26: Exchange of Information
- **Scope of Exchange:** The competent authorities of the U.S. and Japan shall exchange information that is **relevant** for carrying out the provisions of the treaty or the domestic tax laws of either country. This applies to all taxes, not just those covered by the treaty.
- **Secrecy:** Any information received must be treated as secret and can only be disclosed to persons or authorities (including courts and administrative bodies) involved in the assessment, collection, enforcement, or determination of appeals of the taxes in question. It may be disclosed in public court proceedings or judicial decisions.
- **No Obligation to Go Beyond Domestic Law:** A Contracting State is not obligated to:
    - Carry out administrative measures at variance with its own laws/practice.
    - Supply information not obtainable under its own laws or normal administration.
    - Supply information that would disclose a trade secret or be contrary to public policy.
- **Powers to Obtain Information:** Each State must ensure its competent authority has sufficient powers under its domestic law to obtain and exchange the requested information, even if the State does not need the information for its own tax purposes.

### Article 27: Assistance in Collection
- **Obligation to Collect:** Each Contracting State shall endeavor to collect taxes imposed by the other State to ensure that treaty benefits (exemptions or reduced rates) are not enjoyed by persons not entitled to them. The State making the collection is responsible to the other State for the sums collected.
- **Limitation:** A State is not obligated to carry out administrative measures that are at variance with its own laws and administrative practice or that would be contrary to its public policy.

### Article 28: Diplomatic Agents and Consular Officers
- **Saving Clause:** Nothing in this Convention shall affect the fiscal privileges of members of diplomatic missions or consular posts under the general rules of international law or under the provisions of special agreements.

### Article 29: Consultation on Substantial Changes
- **Consultation Right:** If one Contracting State considers that a substantial change in the laws relevant to the treaty has been or will be made in the other State, it may request written consultations to determine the effect on the balance of benefits provided by the treaty and, if appropriate, to amend the treaty to restore an appropriate balance.
- **Timeline:** The requested State must enter into consultations within **three months** from the date the request is received.

### Article 30: Entry into Force
- **Ratification:** The Convention is subject to ratification. The instruments of ratification shall be exchanged as soon as possible. It enters into force on the date of the exchange of instruments of ratification.
- **Applicability Dates:**
    - **In Japan:**
        - *Withholding Tax:* Applies to amounts taxable on or after July 1 of the year of entry into force (if entry is before April 1) or January 1 of the following year (if entry is after March 31).
        - *Other Income Tax/Enterprise Tax:* Applies to income for any taxable year beginning on or after January 1 of the year following entry into force.
    - **In the United States:**
        - *Withholding Tax:* Applies to amounts paid or credited on or after July 1 of the year of entry into force (if entry is before April 1) or January 1 of the following year (if entry is after March 31).
        - *Other Taxes:* Applies to taxable periods beginning on or after January 1 of the year following entry into force.
- **Transition from Prior Treaty:**
    - Individuals entitled to benefits under the prior 1971 Convention (Articles 19 or 20) at the time of entry into force continue to be entitled until they would have ceased under the old treaty.
    - If a person would have been entitled to greater benefits under the prior treaty, they may elect to have the prior Convention continue in effect in its entirety for a period of **twelve months** from the date this new treaty would otherwise take effect.

### Article 31: Termination
- **Duration:** The Convention shall remain in force until terminated by either Contracting State.
- **Termination Notice:** Either State may terminate the Convention after a period of **five years** from the date of its entry into force by giving **six months' prior written notice** through the diplomatic channel.
- **Cessation of Effect:**
    - **In Japan:**
        - *Withholding Tax:* For amounts taxable on or after January 1 of the year following the expiration of the six-month notice period.
        - *Other Taxes:* For income for any taxable year beginning on or after January 1 of the year following the six-month notice period.
    - **In the United States:**
        - *Withholding Tax:* For amounts paid or credited on or after January 1 of the year following the six-month notice period.
        - *Other Taxes:* For taxable periods beginning on or after January 1 of the year following the six-month notice period.
