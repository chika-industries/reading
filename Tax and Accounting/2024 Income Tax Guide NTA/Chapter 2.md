
### Chapter 2 Summary: How to Fill out Your Final Return

This chapter is a detailed, step-by-step guide on physically completing the final tax return form (Page 1 and Page 2). It explains how to calculate your income, claim deductions, and determine your final tax liability.

**2-1 & 2-2: General Instructions**
- The return form is a carbon copy. Write firmly with a black pen.
- Fill in numbers carefully, one digit per box, aligning to the right.
- Special forms ("Page 3" and "Page 4") are needed for separate taxation income or for declaring losses.

**2-3: STEP 1 – Personal Information (Page 1)**
- You must fill in your name, address, occupation, and date of birth.
- You **must** write your **Individual Number (My Number)** . You also need to present or attach copies of identification documents (like your My Number Card or a combination of Notification Card and Driver's License/Passport).
- Other information includes your phone number, the head of your household, and the type of return you are filing (e.g., "Blue Return").

**2-4: STEP 2 – Reporting Income (Page 1)**
This section explains how to calculate the net income for different categories and transfer the amounts to the correct boxes on the form. It covers:
- **Business/Real Estate Income:** Total earnings minus necessary expenses (from your financial statements).
- **Employment Income:** This section includes a table to calculate your "take-home" income after the employment income deduction (e.g., for a salary of 5,000,000 yen, the taxable income is calculated as 5,000,000 × 0.8 - 80,000 = 3,920,000 yen). It also covers the "Exemption of amount of income adjustment."
- **Miscellaneous Income:** A detailed calculation section for income from public pensions and other sources (like side jobs), with different formulas based on age and other income.
- **Capital Gains/Occasional Income:** How to calculate net income after deducting expenses from the sale of assets like gold or from lottery winnings.

**2-5: STEP 3 – Deductions from Income (Page 1 & 2)**
This is the longest section, detailing how to calculate and report various deductions to reduce your taxable income.
- **Social Insurance Premiums:** Deductions for health insurance, pension, etc., paid for yourself or your family.
- **Life and Earthquake Insurance Premiums:** Deductions based on the type and amount of premiums paid, using provided calculation tables.
- **Exemptions for Family Circumstances:** Detailed rules and tables for claiming the:
    - **Spousal Deduction/Special Spousal Deduction:** Based on your income and your spouse's income.
    - **Dependent Deduction:** For qualifying relatives, with higher amounts for specific dependents (e.g., under 23, over 70).
    - **Exemption for Widows/Single Parents/Working Students/Disabled Persons.**
- **Basic Exemption:** A fixed deduction that phases out for higher-income earners (up to 480,000 yen).
- **Deduction for Casualty Losses:** For losses from disasters or theft.
- **Deduction for Medical Expenses:** Calculated as total medical expenses minus reimbursements, minus either 100,000 yen or 5% of your total income. Alternatively, you can choose the **Self-Medication Tax System (Switching to "Deduction for medical expenses by the self- medication taxation system")** deduction for specific over-the-counter drugs.
- **Deduction for Donations:** For donations to the government, certified NPOs, etc. This is where "Furusato Nozei" (hometown tax) donations are reported.

**2-6: STEP 4 – Calculating Your Tax (Page 1)**
- **Taxable Income:** (Total Income from STEP 2) minus (Total Deductions from STEP 3). This amount is rounded down to the nearest 1,000 yen.
- **Base Tax Amount:** Calculated by applying a progressive tax rate (5% to 45%) to your taxable income, using a provided table.
- **Tax Credits:** These are subtracted directly from your base tax amount and include:
    - **Dividend Credit**
    - **Special Credit for Housing Loans**
    - Special Credits for donations to political parties or NPOs
    - Special Credits for earthquake-proof or barrier-free home renovations
- **Special Income Tax for Reconstruction:** After calculating the base income tax, you must add **2.1%** of that amount as the reconstruction tax.
- **Withholding Tax & Final Payment:** The total amount of tax already withheld from your salary or other income is subtracted. The final result is either the **amount you still need to pay** or the **amount to be refunded**.

**2-7 to 2-11: STEP 5 & 6 – Additional Information (Page 1 & 2)**
- **Postponement of Payment:** How to apply to pay part of your tax later.
- **Where to Receive Your Refund:** You must fill in your bank account details (account holder name, branch, account number) for a direct deposit.
- **Public Money Receiving Account:** Option to register your account with the government for receiving future public funds.
- **Inhabitant & Enterprise Taxes (Page 2):** This section is for information that is forwarded to your local municipality. It covers items where local tax rules differ from national tax rules, such as the treatment of small dividends and how your local inhabitant tax will be collected (e.g., deducted from your salary vs. paying it yourself).
