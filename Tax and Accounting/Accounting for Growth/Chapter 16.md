# Summary of Chapter 16: Major Companies Accounting Health Check

This chapter presents the “blob guide”—a simple but remarkably effective tool developed in the original UBS Phillips & Drew report. It lists over 200 major UK quoted companies and indicates which of the 12 accounting and financing techniques (described in earlier chapters) they use. The guide’s power lies in its stark correlation between a high number of “blobs” (techniques used) and subsequent poor share price performance.

## The Techniques Scored

The health check covers 12 categories:

1. **Pre‑acquisition write‑down** – creation of provisions through fair value adjustments.
2. **Disposals (above the line)** – taking disposal profits through the P&L rather than as extraordinary items.
3. **Disposals (deconsolidation)** – ceasing to consolidate a subsidiary ahead of its sale.
4. **Deferred consideration** – earn‑out commitments on acquisitions.
5. **Extraordinary & exceptional items** – classification choices that affect EPS.
6. **Off balance sheet finance** – use of quasi‑subsidiaries, sale & repurchase, joint ventures.
7. **Contingent liabilities** – significant liabilities outside normal course of business.
8. **Capitalisation of costs** – interest, R&D, or other costs turned into assets.
9. **Brand accounting** – capitalisation of brands on the balance sheet.
10. **Changes in depreciation policy** – changes in asset lives or methods.
11. **Convertibles with puts & AMPS** – complex financial instruments.
12. **Pension fund surplus** – using a surplus to reduce P&L charge or create a prepayment asset.

## Key Insight

The blob guide is not a precise scoring system—it cannot rank the severity of each technique. But its simplicity proved powerful: companies that used multiple techniques were far more likely to underperform or fail. As the chapter notes, “Perhaps it should have been called a Wealth Check!”

Investors who avoided companies with high blob counts would have sidestepped some of the most spectacular collapses of the period (Maxwell, LEP, Coloroll, B&C) while still capturing the upside of eventual recoveries like Dixons and Next. The guide underscores the central message of the book: where creative accounting proliferates, shareholder value is often at risk.
