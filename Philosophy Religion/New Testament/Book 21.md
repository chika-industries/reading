Based on the PDF, the next book is the **First Epistle of Peter** (often called **1 Peter**). It is the twenty-first book in the New Testament.

Here is a summary of its content:

- **A Living Hope and Call to Holiness (Chapter 1)** : Peter, an apostle of Jesus Christ, writes to the elect scattered throughout Pontus, Galatia, Cappadocia, Asia, and Bithynia. He blesses God for a living hope through the resurrection of Jesus Christ, an inheritance incorruptible and reserved in heaven. Believers greatly rejoice, though for a season they are in heaviness through manifold temptations. He calls them to be holy in all manner of conversation, not fashioning themselves according to former lusts. They were redeemed not with corruptible silver and gold but with the precious blood of Christ. They are born again by the word of God, which lives and abides forever.

- **The Chosen Stone and a Royal Priesthood (Chapter 2)** : Laying aside all malice, guile, hypocrisies, envies, and evil speakings, believers should desire the sincere milk of the word. Peter quotes scripture about Christ as the chief cornerstone, precious to those who believe but a stone of stumbling to the disobedient. Believers are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, a holy nation. He urges them to abstain from fleshly lusts and to have honest conversation among the Gentiles. They are to submit to every ordinance of man for the Lord’s sake—whether to the king or governors. Servants are to be subject to their masters with all fear, even the froward. Christ suffered for them, leaving an example that they should follow His steps.

- **Wives, Husbands, and Suffering for Righteousness (Chapter 3)** : Wives are to be in subjection to their own husbands, with chaste conversation and a meek and quiet spirit, which is in the sight of God of great price. Husbands are to dwell with their wives according to knowledge, giving honor to them as the weaker vessel. Finally, all should be of one mind, compassionate, loving as brethren, pitiful, courteous, not rendering evil for evil. For he that will love life and see good days must refrain his tongue from evil. It is better to suffer for well-doing than for evil doing. Christ was put to death in the flesh but quickened by the Spirit, by which He went and preached to the spirits in prison.

- **Living for God’s Will and the End Near (Chapter 4)** : Since Christ has suffered in the flesh, believers should arm themselves with the same mind, no longer living to the lusts of men but to the will of God. The end of all things is at hand; therefore be sober, watch unto prayer, have fervent charity among yourselves, use hospitality without grudging, and minister gifts to one another as good stewards of the manifold grace of God. Beloved, think it not strange concerning the fiery trial which is to try you, but rejoice as partakers of Christ’s sufferings. If any suffer as a Christian, let him not be ashamed but glorify God.

- **Instructions to Elders and Final Greetings (Chapter 5)** : Peter exhorts the elders to feed the flock of God, taking oversight not by constraint but willingly, not for filthy lucre but of a ready mind, not as lords over God’s heritage but as examples to the flock. When the chief Shepherd appears, they shall receive a crown of glory. Likewise, younger submit to elder, and all be clothed with humility. Cast all your care upon Him, for He cares for you. Be sober, be vigilant, because your adversary the devil walks about as a roaring lion, seeking whom he may devour. The God of all grace shall perfect, establish, strengthen, and settle them. He sends greetings by Silvanus and from the church in Babylon, and closes with a kiss of charity.

In summary, 1 Peter is a letter of encouragement and exhortation to Christians facing persecution and suffering. Peter reminds them of their living hope through Christ’s resurrection, calls them to holy living, and instructs them on proper conduct in relationships and society. He teaches that suffering for righteousness is honorable and follows Christ’s example, and he urges elders to shepherd God’s flock with humility.
