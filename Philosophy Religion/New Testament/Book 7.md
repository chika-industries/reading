Based on the PDF, the next book is the **First Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Corinthians** (often called **1 Corinthians**). It is the seventh book in the New Testament.

Here is a summary of its content as presented in the document:

- **Address and Thanksgiving (Chapter 1)** : Paul addresses the church at Corinth, sanctified in Christ Jesus, and gives thanks for the grace of God given them. However, he quickly confronts the problem of divisions among them, as some declare loyalty to Paul, others to Apollos, others to Cephas (Peter), and some to Christ. He reminds them that Christ is not divided and that he was sent to preach the gospel, not with wisdom of words, lest the cross of Christ be made of none effect.

- **Paul's Manner of Preaching and God's Wisdom (Chapter 2)** : Paul explains that when he came to them, he did not come with excellency of speech or human wisdom, but declared the testimony of God, determined to know nothing among them except Jesus Christ and Him crucified. He teaches that God's wisdom is revealed by the Spirit to those who are spiritual, which the natural man cannot receive.

- **Carnality, Divisions, and the True Ministry (Chapters 3-4)** : Paul tells them they are yet carnal, as evidenced by their envying, strife, and divisions. He explains that ministers are simply servants by whom they believed, and that God gives the increase. He describes the apostles as fools for Christ's sake, made as the filth of the world, and urges the Corinthians to be followers of him.

- **Judgment of a Fornicator and Dealing with Sin (Chapter 5)** : Paul addresses a report of fornication among them "such as is not so much as named among the Gentiles." He commands them, when gathered together in the name of the Lord, to deliver such a one to Satan for the destruction of the flesh, that the spirit may be saved. He instructs them not to keep company with any man called a brother who is a fornicator, covetous, idolater, railer, drunkard, or extortioner.

- **Lawsuits Among Brethren and the Body as the Temple (Chapter 6)** : Paul rebukes them for going to law before the unjust (unbelievers) instead of settling matters among the saints. He reminds them that the unrighteous shall not inherit the kingdom of God and lists those who will not. He concludes by teaching that the body is not for fornication but for the Lord, and that their bodies are the temple of the Holy Ghost, bought with a price, so they should glorify God in their body.

- **Advice on Marriage and Celibacy (Chapter 7)** : Paul addresses questions about marriage, teaching it is good to avoid fornication. He gives counsel to the unmarried and widows, to husbands and wives, and speaks of his own preference for celibacy while acknowledging that each has his own gift from God. He also counsels believers to abide in the same calling wherein they were called.

- **Food Offered to Idols and Christian Liberty (Chapter 8)** : Paul addresses the question of eating meats offered to idols. He teaches that while an idol is nothing, not everyone has this knowledge. He emphasizes that charity (love) is more important than knowledge, and that believers should be careful not to become a stumbling block to those who are weak, causing them to perish.

In summary, 1 Corinthians is a practical pastoral letter from Paul to a troubled church. He addresses specific problems including divisions, immorality, lawsuits among believers, marriage questions, and disputes over food offered to idols. Throughout, he calls the saints to unity, holiness, and consideration for the weak conscience of others, reminding them that their bodies are temples of the Holy Ghost.
