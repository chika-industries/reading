Based on the PDF, the next book is the **Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Philippians** (often called **Philippians**). It is the eleventh book in the New Testament.

Here is a summary of its content as presented in the document:

- **Paul’s Gratitude and Prayer for the Saints (Chapter 1)** : Paul, with Timothy, greets the saints in Philippi. He gives thanks for their fellowship in the gospel from the first day until now, confident that God who began a good work in them will complete it. He prays that their love may abound in knowledge and judgment. He shares that his imprisonment has actually advanced the gospel, and he expresses that for him to live is Christ and to die is gain.

- **The Mind of Christ and Unity (Chapter 2)** : Paul urges them to be likeminded, having the same love, and to do nothing through strife or vainglory. He presents the profound example of Christ, who, though in the form of God, made Himself of no reputation, took upon Him the form of a servant, and humbled Himself to death on the cross. Therefore God has highly exalted Him. He encourages them to work out their own salvation with fear and trembling, and to shine as lights in the world.

- **Rejoice in the Lord and Press Toward the Mark (Chapters 3-4)** : Paul warns against false teachers who put confidence in the flesh, recounting his own former credentials as a Pharisee. But he counts all things but loss for the excellency of the knowledge of Christ Jesus his Lord. He presses toward the mark for the prize of the high calling of God in Christ. He repeatedly exhorts them to rejoice in the Lord, to be anxious for nothing, and to think on things that are true, honest, just, pure, lovely, and of good report. He concludes with thanks for their support, declaring that God shall supply all their need according to His riches in glory by Christ Jesus.

In summary, Philippians is a joyful and encouraging letter written by Paul from prison. It emphasizes unity, humility, and the example of Christ’s condescension and exaltation. Paul calls the saints to rejoice always, to press forward in their discipleship, and to find their sufficiency in Christ.
