Based on the PDF, the thirteenth book in the New Testament is the **First Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Thessalonians** (often called **1 Thessalonians**). It is the first of two letters Paul wrote to the church in Thessalonica.

Here is a summary of its content:

- **Thanksgiving for Their Faith and Example (Chapter 1)** : Paul, with Silvanus and Timothy, gives thanks for the Thessalonians’ work of faith, labor of love, and patience of hope in Jesus Christ. He notes how they received the word in much affliction with joy of the Holy Ghost, becoming examples to all believers in Macedonia and Achaia.

- **Paul’s Conduct and Their Reception of the Word (Chapter 2)** : Paul reminds them of his visit—how he preached the gospel boldly despite suffering, never using flattery or a cloak of covetousness. He compares his care for them to a nursing mother and a father. He also gives thanks that they received the word not as the word of men but as the word of God.

- **Timothy’s Report and Paul’s Prayer (Chapter 3)** : Concerned about their faith amid persecution, Paul sent Timothy to establish and comfort them. Timothy returns with good news of their faith and love, which greatly encourages Paul. He prays that God will direct his way to them again and that their love may abound.

- **Exhortation to Holiness, Love, and Quiet Living (Chapter 4)** : Paul urges them to walk pleasing to God, abstaining from fornication, and to possess their vessel (body) in sanctification and honor. He emphasizes brotherly love and instructs them to study to be quiet, mind their own business, and work with their hands. He then gives teaching on the Second Coming: the Lord will descend with a shout, the dead in Christ will rise first, and then living believers will be caught up together to meet the Lord.

- **The Day of the Lord and Final Exhortations (Chapter 5)** : Paul explains that the day of the Lord will come like a thief in the night, so believers should be sober, put on faith, love, and the hope of salvation. He gives various instructions: warn the unruly, comfort the feebleminded, support the weak, rejoice evermore, pray without ceasing, give thanks in everything, quench not the Spirit, and hold fast what is good. He concludes with a prayer for their complete sanctification.

In summary, 1 Thessalonians is a warm pastoral letter combining encouragement, doctrinal instruction about the Second Coming, and practical exhortations to live holy and orderly lives. Paul commends the Thessalonians for their steadfast faith and reassures them about the fate of believers who die before the Lord’s return.
