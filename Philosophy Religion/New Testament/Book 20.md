Based on the PDF, the next book is the **Epistle of James** (often called **James**). It is the twentieth book in the New Testament.

Here is a summary of its content:

- **Faith, Patience, and Wisdom (Chapter 1)** : James, a servant of God and of the Lord Jesus Christ, greets the twelve tribes scattered abroad. He teaches that trials and temptations produce patience, which must have its perfect work so that believers may be perfect and entire, wanting nothing. If any lack wisdom, they should ask of God, who gives liberally. Let the lowly brother rejoice in his exaltation, and the rich in his humiliation. Blessed is the man who endures temptation, for he shall receive the crown of life. Do not err, my beloved brethren.

- **Doers of the Word and Respect of Persons (Chapter 2)** : James commands believers to be doers of the word, not hearers only, deceiving their own selves. Pure religion before God is to visit the fatherless and widows in their affliction and to keep oneself unspotted from the world. He strongly warns against showing partiality or respect of persons, such as favoring the rich over the poor. He declares that if a man says he has faith but does not have works, that faith is dead, being alone. Faith without works is dead.

- **The Tongue and Wisdom from Above (Chapter 3)** : James warns that not many should become teachers, for they shall receive greater judgment. The tongue is a small member but boasts great things. It is a fire, a world of iniquity, defiling the whole body. Out of the same mouth proceed blessing and cursing, which should not be so. The wisdom that is from above is first pure, then peaceable, gentle, easy to be entreated, full of mercy and good fruits, without partiality and without hypocrisy.

- **Friendship with the World and Humility (Chapter 4)** : James asks, "From where come wars and fightings among you?" They lust and have not, they kill and desire and cannot obtain. Friendship with the world is enmity with God. Submit yourselves therefore to God. Resist the devil, and he will flee from you. Draw near to God, and He will draw near to you. Humble yourselves in the sight of the Lord, and He shall lift you up. Do not boast about tomorrow, for life is a vapor. To him who knows to do good and does it not, to him it is sin.

- **Warnings to the Rich and Exhortations to Patience and Prayer (Chapter 5)** : James warns rich men who have heaped treasure together for the last days, who have withheld wages from laborers. He urges believers to be patient until the coming of the Lord, like the farmer waits for the precious fruit of the earth. Take the prophets as an example of suffering and patience. Above all, do not swear, but let your yes be yes. If any are afflicted, let them pray. If any are sick, let them call for the elders of the church, and let them pray over him, anointing with oil in the name of the Lord, and the prayer of faith shall save the sick. Confess faults one to another and pray for one another. He who converts a sinner from the error of his way shall save a soul from death.

In summary, the Epistle of James is a practical, wisdom-filled letter emphasizing that genuine faith must be demonstrated by good works. It covers topics such as enduring trials, controlling the tongue, avoiding partiality, seeking wisdom from God, resisting worldliness, and the power of prayer. James strongly teaches that faith without works is dead, making it one of the most action-oriented books in the New Testament.
