Based on the PDF, the next book is the **Second Epistle of Peter** (often called **2 Peter**). It is the twenty-second book in the New Testament.

Here is a summary of its content:

- **Grace, Knowledge, and the Divine Power (Chapter 1)** : Peter writes to those who have obtained precious faith through the righteousness of God. He declares that God’s divine power has given them all things that pertain to life and godliness. He lists the steps of Christian growth: add to faith virtue, to virtue knowledge, to knowledge temperance, to temperance patience, to patience godliness, to godliness brotherly kindness, and to brotherly kindness charity (love). He states that he will shortly put off his earthly tabernacle (die), and he wants them to be reminded. He affirms that he was an eyewitness of Christ’s majesty on the mount of transfiguration, and he declares that prophecy came not by the will of man but by holy men of God speaking as they were moved by the Holy Ghost.

- **False Prophets and Their Destruction (Chapter 2)** : Peter warns that false prophets will arise among the people, bringing in damnable heresies, even denying the Lord. Many will follow their pernicious ways, and the way of truth will be evil spoken of. He describes these false teachers as covetous, feigned with words, walking after the flesh, despising government, presumptuous, self-willed, and speaking evil of dignities. He uses examples of God’s judgment: angels who sinned were cast down to hell, the old world was destroyed by flood (saving Noah), and Sodom and Gomorrah were condemned (delivering Lot). These false teachers are wells without water, clouds carried by a tempest, promising liberty but themselves servants of corruption.

- **The Lord’s Coming and Scoffers (Chapter 3)** : Peter writes this second epistle to stir up their pure minds, reminding them of the words of the holy prophets and the commandments of the Lord. In the last days, scoffers will come, walking after their own lusts, asking, “Where is the promise of His coming?” They forget that the heavens and earth were created by God’s word, and that the world perished by water. The present heavens and earth are reserved for fire against the day of judgment. Beloved, be not ignorant that one day is with the Lord as a thousand years, and a thousand years as one day. The Lord is not slack concerning His promise, but is longsuffering, not willing that any should perish but that all should come to repentance. The day of the Lord will come as a thief in the night. Therefore, since all these things shall be dissolved, what manner of persons ought they to be in holy conversation and godliness? They should be diligent to be found of Him in peace, without spot and blameless. He concludes with a warning about Paul’s writings, which some wrest to their own destruction, and an exhortation to grow in grace and knowledge of the Lord Jesus Christ.

In summary, 2 Peter is a final testament from the apostle Peter, written shortly before his death. It emphasizes the importance of growing in spiritual virtues, warns against false teachers who would lead believers astray, and addresses scoffers who deny the Lord’s second coming. Peter affirms that God is not slow but patient, and that the day of the Lord will come with fire. He urges believers to live holy lives and to be diligent to be found in peace.
