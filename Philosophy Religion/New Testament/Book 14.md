Based on the PDF, the next book is the **Second Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Thessalonians** (often called **2 Thessalonians**). It is the fourteenth book in the New Testament.

Here is a summary of its content:

- **Thanksgiving for Their Faith and Patience (Chapter 1)** : Paul, along with Silvanus and Timothy, greets the Thessalonian saints. He gives thanks for their growing faith and abounding love, and he boasts of their patience and faith amid persecutions and tribulations. He assures them that God will judge their persecutors and give rest to the afflicted when the Lord Jesus is revealed from heaven with His mighty angels.

- **The Day of the Lord and the Man of Sin (Chapter 2)** : Paul addresses confusion about the coming of Christ. He urges them not to be troubled by false claims that the day of the Lord has already arrived. He explains that a falling away (apostasy) must come first, and the “man of sin” (the son of perdition) will be revealed—one who opposes and exalts himself above all that is called God. Paul reminds them that they are chosen to salvation through sanctification of the Spirit and belief of the truth, and encourages them to stand fast in the traditions they were taught.

- **Exhortation to Prayer, Discipline, and Work (Chapter 3)** : Paul asks for their prayers that the word of the Lord may spread quickly. He expresses confidence in the Lord that they will continue to obey. He then gives a direct command: if anyone refuses to work, neither should they eat. He urges them to withdraw from brothers who walk disorderly and not according to tradition. The letter concludes with a prayer for peace and a final greeting in Paul’s own hand.

In summary, 2 Thessalonians is a follow‑up letter that corrects misunderstandings about the timing of the Second Coming. Paul clarifies that certain events—including a widespread apostasy and the emergence of the “man of sin”—must occur before the Lord’s return. He also reinforces the importance of faithful work and orderly living within the community.
